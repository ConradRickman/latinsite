Latin Syntax

In English, we follow a somewhat strict word order when constructing sentences. Your sentences must be in a subject-verb-object order. For example the English sentence "The dog loves the boy," dog is the subject, runs is the verb, and boy is the object. You can not write the sentence "The dog the boy runs," (Subject-Object-Verb) in English, and if you change the order of the subject and object, you create a completely different meaning "The boy loves the dog."

Latin has no fixed word order. Technically, words could be in any order and still have a gramatically correct sentence. If you wanted to write the "The dog loves the boy," in Latin, you would typically say, "canis puerum amat," but you could technically put the words for dog, love, and boy in any order. To know what the subject and object are, you need to look at the endings of each word. Because Latin is an inflected language the endings of words change depending on their function in the sentence. This is why learning your declentions and conjugations is important! 📜
